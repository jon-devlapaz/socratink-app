# Theta Research: Tetris Effect and Learning Science (2026-04-17)

## Summary

Neurocognitive research note produced by the theta agent in response to the founder question: "can we elicit the Tetris effect for Socratink?" The note covers mechanism, evidence base, failure modes, and product framing implications.

The core finding is that the Tetris effect (Stickgold et al. 2000, *Science*) is a real phenomenon but its mechanism is procedural/implicit memory — not the hippocampal-dependent declarative network that carries Socratink's conceptual knowledge. The amnesic-patient dissociation is the clean empirical anchor: patients with bilateral MTL damage showed hypnagogic Tetris imagery despite no episodic recall of playing. That dissociation is exactly what makes the Tetris effect striking, and it is also what prevents the mechanism from mapping cleanly onto declarative learning. "Tetris effect" therefore holds as a product metaphor and copywriting device, but cannot be stated as the underlying mechanism for Socratink in product messaging.

The evidence-backed substrate for schema-leak-style benefits in Socratink is spaced retrieval practice (confirmed, g = 0.50–0.61 vs re-study, with vmPFC neuroimaging 2025), generation-before-recognition / generation effect (confirmed, McCurdy 2020 meta-analysis), context-varied / interleaved drill (likely, moderate evidence), and post-session rest / consolidation windows (likely, from broader consolidation literature). The note confirms that the existing GBR doctrine sits on the right mechanistic footing and surfaces three failure modes that Socratink should actively avoid: schema narrowing from repeated massed drill in one context, overlearning rigidity, and parallel overload across too many concurrent concepts.

The note's design implications — vary drill context across sessions, interleave across nodes in a cluster, and surface a post-session "Where could you see this today?" anchor prompt — are framed explicitly as hypotheses grounded in adjacent evidence, not validated product decisions. This is foundational learning-science context, not a release-gate item, so it stays in the wiki and does not enter `ACTIVE.md`.
