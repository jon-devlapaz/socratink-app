# Desirable Difficulty Research Note

## Summary

This source is the 2026-04-18 theta primary-literature review of the desirable-difficulty framework — the umbrella theoretical frame under which Socratink's Generation-Before-Recognition doctrine, unscored cold attempt, and spaced-re-drill-only-solidification invariants all sit.

The note anchors the framework in Bjork (1994, the original term), Bjork & Bjork (1992) *New Theory of Disuse* — storage strength vs. retrieval strength, with the critical asymmetry that storage strength gains are largest when retrieval strength is low but non-zero — and Bjork & Bjork (2011) as the canonical modern statement. The framework is taxonomic, not monolithic: spacing, interleaving, testing/retrieval, generation, varying conditions of practice, and reduced/delayed feedback are each independently evidence-backed. Schmidt & Bjork (1992) and Soderstrom & Bjork (2015) establish the load-bearing learning-vs-performance distinction that makes the unscored cold attempt principled rather than arbitrary.

The note strongly supports four Socratink doctrines with primary-literature citation: unscored cold attempt (because during-acquisition performance is a miscalibrated signal of storage strength); solidified-only-from-spaced-re-drill (direct application of New Theory of Disuse); Generation Before Recognition (one of six canonical desirable difficulties); and Socratic questioning as reduced-feedback scaffolding (Schmidt & Bjork 1992).

Four constraints and failure modes are flagged as first-class product risks: (1) expertise reversal (Kalyuga et al. 2003) directly contradicts cold-attempt-first for zero-schema learners on high-element-interactivity material — Socratink's cold attempt is miscalibrated for that subset; (2) metacognitive resistance (Kornell & Bjork 2008; Kirk-Johnson et al. 2019) is a UX risk, not just an empirical one — learners reliably prefer fluent practice and churn from hard-but-valuable sessions; (3) perceptual/UI disfluency is not a desirable difficulty (post-2015 non-replication — Rummer 2016; Geller 2018); UI friction should be ruthlessly eliminated while cognitive-operation friction is preserved; (4) the "desirable" line is learner- and material-specific and not prospectively computable — product design cannot pre-compute the difficulty setpoint.

The note's instrumentation proposal identifies four empirical tests that would confirm the framework is operating in Socratink: delayed retention gap at +7d/+30d controlled for study time; confidence-correctness calibration improving over time; abandonment-vs-difficulty correlation as the Kornell & Bjork churn signature; schema-availability moderator tagging first-time-in-domain vs. extension-of-known concepts.
